Insurance Terminology

Accident – An unexpected event causing loss or damage.

Actual Cash Value (ACV) – The value of an item after accounting for depreciation.

Actuarial Science – The discipline that applies statistical and mathematical methods to assess risk.

Actuary – A professional who calculates insurance risks and premiums.

Adverse Selection – When high-risk individuals are more likely to purchase insurance than low-risk ones.

Aggregate Limit – The maximum amount an insurer will pay for all claims in a policy period.

Agent – A person who sells insurance policies on behalf of an insurer.

Aleatory Contract – A contract where performance depends on an uncertain event (e.g., insurance payouts).

Appraisal – A professional assessment of the value of an item or damage for claims.

Assumed Risk – A risk knowingly taken by a policyholder.

At-Fault Accident – An accident where the policyholder is determined responsible.

Binder – A temporary insurance policy providing coverage until a formal policy is issued.

Broker – A person who represents insurance buyers, rather than the insurance company.

Cancellation – Termination of an insurance policy before its expiration date.

Captive Insurance Company – An insurer owned by the insured entity for self-insurance purposes.

Claim Adjuster – A professional who assesses claims to determine payouts.

Claimant – A person who submits a claim for benefits under an insurance policy.

Coinsurance Clause – A provision requiring the policyholder to share costs based on a percentage.

Comprehensive Coverage – Covers damage to a vehicle not caused by a collision.

Contingent Liability – A liability that depends on the outcome of an uncertain event.

Coverage Limit – The maximum amount an insurer will pay for a covered loss.

Declarations Page – A summary of an insurance policy’s key terms and conditions.

Deductible – The amount a policyholder must pay before insurance coverage applies.

Direct Loss – Physical damage or financial loss covered by an insurance policy.

Effective Date – The date when coverage begins.

Endorsement (Rider) – A modification to a standard insurance policy.

Excess Coverage – Insurance coverage that kicks in after primary coverage is exhausted.

Exclusion – Situations or damages not covered by an insurance policy.

Experience Rating – A method of determining premiums based on past claims history.

Fraud – Intentional deception to obtain insurance benefits.

Grace Period – The extra time after a missed payment before a policy is canceled.

Hazard – A factor that increases the likelihood of a loss occurring.

Indemnity – Compensation for loss or damage.

Inland Marine Insurance – Covers movable property, such as goods in transit.

Insurance Pooling – Spreading risk across a large group of policyholders.

Insurable Interest – A financial stake in the subject of an insurance policy.

Lapse – Termination of coverage due to non-payment.

Liability – Legal responsibility for causing injury or damage.

Loss Ratio – The percentage of premiums paid out as claims.

Named Peril Policy – A policy covering only specific risks listed in the contract.

No-Fault Insurance – An auto insurance system where each driver’s insurer covers their damages.

Occurrence – An event that results in an insurance claim.

Peril – A specific cause of loss (e.g., fire, theft, flood).

Policyholder – The person or entity who owns an insurance policy.

Premium – The amount paid for an insurance policy.

Primary Coverage – The first layer of coverage that pays before any other policies apply.

Proof of Loss – Documentation submitted to support a claim.

Reinsurance – Insurance for insurance companies, spreading risk among multiple insurers.

Renewal – The process of continuing an insurance policy after its term ends.

Rider – An additional provision to an insurance policy.

Salvage Value – The estimated value of damaged property.

Self-Insurance – When a company or individual sets aside funds to cover potential losses instead of purchasing insurance.

Subrogation – The insurer’s right to recover costs from a third party responsible for a loss.

Third-Party Insurance – Coverage for damage or injury caused to others.

Underwriting – The process of evaluating risk to determine insurance eligibility and pricing.

Allowed Amount – The maximum payment an insurer will approve for a medical service.

Annual Limit – The cap on total benefits an insurance policy will pay in a year.

Balance Billing – When a provider bills the patient for the amount not covered by insurance.

Catastrophic Coverage – A policy covering only severe, high-cost medical expenses.

COBRA – A federal law allowing individuals to continue employer-based coverage after leaving a job.

Co-insurance – The percentage of medical costs a patient must pay after the deductible is met.

Coordination of Benefits (COB) – A rule determining which insurance policy pays first when multiple coverages apply.

Drug Formulary – A list of medications covered by a health plan.

Essential Health Benefits – A set of healthcare services that must be covered under the ACA.

Health Savings Account (HSA) – A tax-advantaged account for medical expenses.

Medicare Part A/B/C/D – The different components of Medicare covering hospital, medical, and prescription costs.

Out-of-Pocket Maximum – The maximum a patient pays in a year before insurance covers all remaining costs.

Pre-existing Condition – A medical condition existing before insurance coverage starts.

Utilization Review – The process of evaluating the necessity of medical services.

Accident Report – A document detailing the circumstances of an accident.

Auto Liability Coverage – Covers damage or injuries caused by a driver to others.

Comprehensive Coverage – Protects against non-collision damage, such as theft or weather-related events.

Endorsement – A policy modification adding or changing coverage terms.

Loan/Lease Payoff Coverage – Covers the gap between a car’s actual value and the remaining loan balance.

Medical Payments (MedPay) – Covers medical costs for the driver and passengers regardless of fault.

Personal Injury Protection (PIP) – Covers medical expenses and lost wages after an accident.

Salvage Title – A title issued to vehicles that have been declared total losses.

SR-22 Insurance – A certificate proving financial responsibility for high-risk drivers.

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage – Pays for damages if the other driver lacks sufficient coverage.

Additional Insured – A person added to a policy for coverage.

Dwelling Policy – A policy specifically for structures, not contents.

Fair Rental Value Coverage – Reimburses lost rental income if a property is uninhabitable.

HO-3 Policy – The most common homeowners insurance covering home, liability, and personal property.

Inflation Guard – Adjusts coverage limits based on inflation.

Loss of Use Coverage – Pays for living expenses if a home is temporarily uninhabitable.

Scheduled Personal Property – Extra coverage for high-value items like jewelry and art.

Accelerated Death Benefit – Allows early payout if the policyholder is terminally ill.

Convertible Term Life Insurance – Allows switching from term to permanent coverage.

Death Benefit – The money paid to a beneficiary upon the policyholder’s death.

Guaranteed Issue Policy – A policy requiring no medical exam for approval.

Term Life Insurance – Coverage for a specific period, usually 10-30 years.

Whole Life Insurance – Permanent insurance with guaranteed premiums and cash value.

Business Interruption Insurance – Covers lost income due to disasters.

Commercial Property Insurance – Covers buildings and equipment.

Directors and Officers (D&O) Insurance – Protects executives from lawsuits.

Errors and Omissions (E&O) Insurance – Covers professional mistakes and negligence.

Cyber Liability Insurance – Protects businesses from data breaches and cybercrime.

Event Cancellation Insurance – Covers losses if an event is canceled.

Pet Insurance – Covers veterinary expenses.

Travel Insurance – Protects against trip cancellations and medical emergencies
